Open Rhotano Sea at Evening

Spectral: ✓ Bite: ! Bait: Plump (Spectral Bass), Ragworm (Lampfish, Silencer)

Strategy

  • According to data from Cat Became Hungry, Lampish are relatively rare.
  • As of 5.4, using Double Hook on Silencer yields 4 instead of 2. Do not blind double hook as the pool is too polluted even though evening weather removes a number of ! spectral fish.

Outer Rothlyt Sound at Night

Spectral: ? Bite: ! Bait: Plump (Spectresaur), Ragworm (Garum Jug, Crow Puffer, Honeycomb Fish), Krill (Pearl Bombfish)

Strategy

  • Ginkgo Fin is included in the line timers table because it is possible to catch with ragworms. However, you cannot get this without the intuition.
  • Long ! bites in normal weather can be double-hooked blind for 4 Honeycomb Fish.
  • If you don't have enough GP for a double hook, consider doing short casts for crow puffers.
  • It is not confirmed if Pearl Bombfish can be caught with ragworms.
  • I am not certain that a spectral is necessary for this area. The split bait between puffers combined with low catch rates compare to Crepe Soles and Rothlyt Mussels make me think relying on Honeycombs is the most ideal scenario. (Data: Cat Became Hungry)
